Highlights of G. Villeneuve's life:

Joseph Gilles Henri Villeneuve (January 18, 1950 – May 8, 1982), known as Gilles Villeneuve, was a Canadian F1 racing driver. Villeneuve spent six years in Grand Prix racing with Ferrari, winning six races and widespread acclaim for his performances.

  • 1947 - His parents, Séville and Georgette Villeneuve, live in Chambly, Canada.
  • 1950 - Birth on January 18 at the Saint-Jean-sur-Richelieu Hospital, Quebec.
  • 1953 - Birth of his brother Jacques.
  • 1955 - Elementary School beginning at Chambly.
  • 1965 - Father gave Gilles an old 1958 MGA sports car which he had bought for $100.
  • 1958 - The family moved to Berthierville, Quebec.
  • 1966 - Ends school at Sainte-Rose. Gets driving licence and his first car, a Skoda.
  • 1970 - Wedding with Joann Barthe on the October 17.
  • 1971 - Birth of his first child Jacques, future F1 driver.
  • 1973 - Birth of his second child, Mélanie. Beginning in Formula Ford. Won the Quebec Championship.
  • 1974 - Won the world championship of snowmobile at Eagle River, Wisconsin (USA).
  • 1975 - Won his first victory at Gimli, Manitoba.
  • 1976 - Won nine races over 10 in Atlantic Formula. Obtained the Canadian and American Championships.
  • 1977 - Discovered and introduced to F1 by James Hunt, Villeneuve made his debut for McLaren at Silverstone.
  • 1978 - He scored his first Grand Prix win on home ground at Montreal, October, 8.
  • 1979 - Finished second in the championship to teammate Jody Scheckter.
  • 1981 - Fifth victory at the Monaco Grand Prix in May. Sixth victory in June at the Jarama Grand Prix, Spain.
  • 1982 - Fatal accident at the second day of the qualifications at the Zolder Grand Prix, Belgium.

The end of Legend

Villeneuve died in a 140 mph (225 km/h) crash caused by a collision with the March of Jochen Mass during qualifying for the 1982 Belgian Grand Prix at Zolder. The accident came less than two weeks after an intense argument with his teammate, Didier Pironi, over Pironi's move to pass Villeneuve at the preceding San Marino Grand Prix. At the time of his death, Villeneuve was extremely popular with fans and has since become an iconic figure in the history of the sport. His son, Jacques Villeneuve, became Formula One world champion in 1997 and, to date, the only Canadian to win the F1 Championship.

Gilles did not win any championships, but he won the respect and admiration of F1 lovers until the end of time...
